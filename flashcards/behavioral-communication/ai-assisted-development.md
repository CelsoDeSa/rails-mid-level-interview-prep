# AI-Assisted Development

## Question

How much do you rely on AI when coding, and how will you handle coding without AI during the interview?

## Brief Answer

AI accelerates implementation, research, refactoring ideas, and boilerplate, but I still own architecture, review, testing, debugging, and final decisions. For the interview I will think out loud and code without AI.

## Comprehensive Explanation

A strong answer is honest but confident:

> “I use AI heavily as a productivity tool, especially for scaffolding, exploring options, generating tests, and speeding up routine implementation. But I still own the code: I review it, adapt it, test it, debug it, and make the trade-off decisions. I understand that in this interview I won’t use AI, so I’ll focus on thinking out loud, explaining the approach, and writing straightforward code.”

Avoid implying that AI writes code you cannot explain. Emphasize independent reasoning, production responsibility, and validation.
